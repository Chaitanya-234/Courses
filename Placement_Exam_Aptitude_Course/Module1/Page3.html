Topic: Percentages

Concept Overview

Percentage is a method of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It is denoted using the '%' sign. Percentages are fundamental to understanding Profit & Loss, Interest, Data Interpretation, and many other topics. Mastering percentages is a key skill for quantitative aptitude.

1. Basic Percentage Calculation

To find x% of a number N, the formula is: Value = (x/100) × N.

2. Percentage Increase and Decrease

When a value grows from Initial to Final, the percentage increase is ((Final - Initial) / Initial) × 100.

3. Successive Percentage Change

When a value changes by x% and then by y%, the net percentage change is given by the formula: Net Change % = x + y + (xy/100).

4. Applications in Real-Life Problems

Percentages are ubiquitous in finance, sales, and statistics.

Detailed Explanation

Product Constancy Rule:If the product of two variables is constant, and one variable changes by x%, the other must change inversely to keep the product constant.

Real-World Examples

Salary Increment.

Practice Drill

Solve 20 problems on Percentage Increase, Decrease, and Successive Change.

Key Takeaways

  • Master the fundamental definitions and properties.
  • Practice applying formulas in different contexts.
  • Focus on accuracy first, then speed.
  • Review solved examples to understand the logic.
