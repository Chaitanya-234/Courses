Time, Speed, and Distance

Concept Overview

Time, Speed, and Distance includes problems on trains, boats and streams, and races. Understanding the relationship between these variables and relative speed is key.

1. Basic Formulas

  • Distance = Speed Ã— Time
  • Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time

2. Relative Speed

  • Same Direction: Relative Speed = S1 - S2.
  • Opposite Direction: Relative Speed = S1 + S2.

3. Problems on Trains

  • Train passes a pole: Distance = Length of Train.
  • Train passes a platform: Distance = Length of Train + Length of Platform.

4. Boats and Streams

  • Downstream Speed: u + v.
  • Upstream Speed: u - v.

Real-World Examples

Scenario: Flight Scheduling

A plane flies against a wind of 40 km/h for 3 hours. On return with the wind, it takes 2 hours. Find speed.

3(u-40) = 2(u+40). u = 200 km/h.
