Ratio and Proportion

Concept Overview

Ratio and Proportion are fundamental concepts for comparing quantities. They form the basis for topics like mixtures, partnership, time and work, and geometry. A strong grasp of ratios simplifies complex problems into manageable units.

1. Ratio Concepts

A ratio is a comparison of two or more quantities of the same kind by division. It is denoted by the colon symbol (:).

  • Antecedent: The first term 'a'.
  • Consequent: The second term 'b'.

Example: If there are 10 boys and 15 girls in a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 10:15, which simplifies to 2:3.

Properties of Ratios

  • A ratio remains unchanged if both terms are multiplied or divided by the same non-zero number. (2:3 = 4:6 = 20:30).
  • Compounded Ratio: If ratios are a:b and c:d, the compounded ratio is (ac):(bd).
  • Duplicate Ratio: The duplicate ratio of a:b is a²:b².
  • Triplicate Ratio: The triplicate ratio of a:b is a³:b³.

2. Direct and Inverse Proportion

Proportion states that two ratios are equal. Written as a:b :: c:d or a/b = c/d.

  • Product of Extremes = Product of Means: a × d = b × c.

Direct Proportion

Two quantities are in direct proportion if an increase in one leads to a proportional increase in the other. (x/y = k).

Example: Cost of items vs. Quantity purchased.

Inverse Proportion

Two quantities are in inverse proportion if an increase in one leads to a proportional decrease in the other. (x × y = k).

Example: Speed vs. Time taken for a fixed distance. If speed doubles, time halves.

3. Continued Proportion

Three quantities a, b, c are in continued proportion if a:b = b:c.

  • Mean Proportional: b is the mean proportional between a and c. b² = ac, or b = √ac.
  • Third Proportional: c is the third proportional to a and b. c = b²/a.

4. Partnership Problems

Partnership deals with the division of profit or loss among partners based on their investment and time period.

Principle: Profit Ratio = (Investment of A × Time of A) : (Investment of B × Time of B).

Example: A invests $1000 for 12 months. B invests $2000 for 6 months.

Ratio of Profits = (1000 × 12) : (2000 × 6) = 12000 : 12000 = 1:1.

Detailed Explanation: Mixtures and Allegations

Allegation is a rule to find the ratio in which two or more ingredients at given prices must be mixed to produce a mixture of a desired price.

Rule of Allegation:

Quantity of Cheaper / Quantity of Dearer = (Price of Dearer - Mean Price) / (Mean Price - Price of Cheaper)

Example: In what ratio must rice at $10/kg be mixed with rice at $15/kg to get a mixture worth $12/kg?

Mean Price = 12. Cheaper = 10. Dearer = 15.

Ratio = (15 - 12) : (12 - 10) = 3 : 2.

Real-World Examples

Scenario: Recipe Scaling

A recipe for 4 people requires 2 cups of flour and 1 cup of sugar. How much is needed for 10 people?

Solution: The ratio of people is 10:4 = 5:2 or 2.5.

Flour needed = 2 cups × 2.5 = 5 cups.

Sugar needed = 1 cup × 2.5 = 2.5 cups.
