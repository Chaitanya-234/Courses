Topic: Arithmetic Operations and Simplification

Concept Overview

Simplification is the process of reducing a complex mathematical expression into a simpler form. It is the most frequently tested topic in quantitative aptitude exams. Mastery over basic arithmetic operations and the order of operations is essential for speed and accuracy.

1. BODMAS Rule

The fundamental rule for solving mathematical expressions involving multiple operations is BODMAS.

2. Fractions and Decimals

Understanding conversions between fractions and decimals is crucial for quick calculations.

3. Surds and Indices

Surds are irrational roots of numbers, while Indices (exponents) represent repeated multiplication.

4. Approximation and Estimation

In many aptitude questions, exact calculation is time-consuming. Approximation techniques help in eliminating options quickly.

Detailed Explanation

Square Roots and Cube Roots:Memorizing squares up to 30 and cubes up to 15 is highly recommended for speed.
