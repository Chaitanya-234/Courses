Time and Work

Concept Overview

Time and Work is about relating the amount of work done to the time taken and the efficiency of the workers. This topic is logically linked to Inverse Proportion.

1. Fundamental Concept

Work = Rate × Time

  • Rate (Efficiency): Work done per unit time.
  • Time: Total time taken to complete the work.
  • Work: Often assumed to be 1 unit (the whole job) or LCM of days.

2. Work and Wages

Wages are distributed in proportion to the work done, not the time taken.

Ratio of Wages = Ratio of Work Done = Ratio of Efficiency × Ratio of Time.

3. Pipes and Cisterns

This is identical to Time and Work, except for the concept of a leak or outlet pipe.

  • Inlet Pipe: Fills the tank (Positive work).
  • Outlet Pipe (Leak): Empties the tank (Negative work).

Detailed Explanation: Efficiency Relationships

If A is x times as efficient as B, then ratio of work done in same time = x:1.

Ratio of time taken to do same work = 1:x.

Real-World Examples

Scenario: Project Planning

12 men can complete a project in 18 days. After 6 days, 4 men leave. How long will the remaining men take to finish?

Solution: Total Work = 12 × 18 = 216 man-days.

Work done = 12 × 6 = 72.

Remaining = 144. Remaining Men = 8.

Time = 144 / 8 = 18 days.
