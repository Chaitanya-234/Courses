Data Sufficiency

Concept Overview

Data Sufficiency questions ask whether the given data is sufficient to answer the question, not what the answer is.

1. Question Structure

Statement I and II. Options A (I only), B (II only), C (Either), D (Neither), E (Both).

2. Strategy

  1. Evaluate I alone.
  2. Evaluate II alone.
  3. Combine if needed.

3. Common Pitfalls

Don't assume integers or positive numbers unless specified. Ensure the answer is unique.
