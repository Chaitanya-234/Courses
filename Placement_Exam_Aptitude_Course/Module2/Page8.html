Statement and Conclusion

Concept Overview

Statement and Conclusion questions evaluate your ability to analyze a given statement and identify the underlying assumptions or logical inferences.

1. Assumptions

An assumption is something supposed or taken for granted. It links the unsaid parts.

2. Conclusions

A conclusion is a deduction drawn from the statement. It must be strictly based on information provided.

3. Course of Action

Identify practical steps to solve a problem presented in the statement.

4. Strong and Weak Arguments

Strong arguments are logical and relevant. Weak arguments are vague or emotional.
